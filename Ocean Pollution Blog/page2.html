Why

Is There Ocean Pollution?

Ocean pollution is a direct result from human behavior. Examples of pollution are littering, runoff from agriculture, oil spills, and sewage. Most pollution found in the ocean is plastic which is used in many man-made products like canned food, toys, toothbrushes, and other common items. Oil spills are caused by accidents or carelessness from drilling, tankers, pipelines, and many factories.

Basically, what I'm saying is, it's all our fault.






Who

Does This Affect?

Ocean pollution affects most animals in the water/land and indirectly harms humans. According to Ocean Conservancy, "Plastic has been found in 59% of sea birds like albatross and pelicans, in 100% of sea turtle species, and more than 25% of fish sampled from seafood markets around the world." Trash like plastic can harm wildlife through entanglement or consumption. When fish and other animals consume plastic, they eat the contaminants that the plastic absorbed and could eventually die. Land animals can also be hurt with garbage such as broken bottles on the beach. Small birds who have eaten cigarettes have been starved to death because of the nicotine.




Biology lesson taught from my freshman teacher about chain reactions from pollution---Runoff from agriculture (fertilizer/pesticide) affects the phosphorus cycle in fresh water ecosystems. With an abundance of phosphorus,the amount of algae grow rapidly but eventually die after using up all the phosphate. Dead algae are then eaten by bacteria whose population also increases. All the bacteria need to consume oxygen in the water which depletes the amount so much that other organisms like fishes suffocate. This process is called eutrophication. Therefore, the enviromment is less diverse and more vulnerable to changes in the ecosystem (natural disasters).




How does this affect humans? When people eat fish that has eaten trash, they are also being contaminated with the trash. In an article written by Andrew Dilevics on Planet Aid, three important roles that the ocean plays in our ecosystem are providing oxygen, supplying our water, and absorbing our carbon dioxide emissions. When the ocean is filled with mercury and other chemicals due to pollution, this can lead to dangerous human health issues from exposure to said contaminants. These contaminants can cause kidney damage, issues in the human reproductive system, and harmful effects to the nervous system.
